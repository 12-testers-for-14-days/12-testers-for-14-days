    FAQ

    Questions? Answered.

    Still have questions? Chat with us
    Are these real humans or automated bots?
    100% real human testers on physical Android devices. We never use bots or emulators for google play console testing. Google's systems easily detect automation. You can verify all device models directly in your closed track dashboard.
    What if Google rejects my production access?
    If rejected due to the testing phase, we repeat the full 14-day cycle at zero cost. If it fails a second time, you receive a 100% refund. Check out our guide on reasons Google rejects production access to avoid policy-related rejections.
    How fast does closed testing start?
    Most orders have testers assigned within 12–24 hours of payment. Testers will appear in your Play Console the same day you order.
    Do you need my APK or developer account login?
    No. You only need to share your app's package name or internal/closed testing track link from the Play Console. Learn how to set up your closed testing track here. We never access your developer account.
    Do testers actually use the app every day?
    Yes, this is critical. Play Console testing tracks active session data, not just install counts. Our testers engage with your app daily for all 14 days, which you can verify in your dashboard.
    What happens after 14 days complete?
    We send you a clear, step-by-step guide on how to apply for access to production. Most approvals arrive within 3–7 business days, allowing you to publish globally.
    Will testers leave feedback in the Play Console?
    Yes. Having testers leave structured feedback is a vital part of successfully completing the closed track. Our testers submit genuine, actionable feedback through Google's native feedback system, creating a verifiable record for when you apply for access to production.
    Why shouldn't I just use free testing groups?
    Free groups on Reddit or Facebook suffer from massive drop-off rates. If even one person uninstalls before the two weeks are up, your counter resets. Read our comparison of free ways to get 12 real testers to see why investing in guaranteed retention saves you weeks of frustration.
    Can I push app updates during the 14-day test?
    Absolutely. You can push updates to your closed testing track while the test is running. In fact, Google encourages active development. Pushing an update will not reset your 14-day continuous testing clock.
    Where are your testers located geographically?
    Our testers are globally distributed across various regions, networks, and carriers. This diversity is exactly what Google looks for to simulate a natural, healthy app launch during Play Console testing.
    What types of Android apps do you test?
    We test almost everything: games, SaaS dashboards, utility tools, health apps, and e-commerce platforms. As long as your app does not violate core Google Play policies and does not require highly specialized hardware, we can get 12 testers for 14 days assigned to it.
    Can I test multiple apps at the exact same time?
    Yes. If you are a studio or agency, we highly recommend our Growth or Agency plans. We can run parallel testing on multiple apps simultaneously, providing you with a dedicated account manager to streamline your path to production.
